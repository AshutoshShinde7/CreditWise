Best Credit Cards in India for Online Shopping 2023

Credit cards are a popular way to make purchases, especially for people who don't have the money on hand to pay for an item. They're also useful for those who want to buy something but don't have enough time to save up the money before they need it--for example if you want a new laptop and know that it will take several months of saving up before you can afford it.

The name "credit card" comes from their ability to give consumers access to credit (or borrowed funds), which allows them more flexibility when making purchases than cash would allow. Credit cards are issued by banks or other financial institutions and can be used at any location that accepts them as payment.

How Credit Cards Make Online Shopping Easier?

Credit cards are the most convenient way to shop online. They offer a number of benefits that make it easier for you to make purchases, including:

  • Security features like fraud protection and chargeback rights
  • Convenience in making payments online or in-store
  • Credit cards also provide added protection against fraudulent charges and identity theft, as well as easy access to customer service when something goes wrong with your order.

Top 7 Credit Cards in India

1. HDFC Millenia Credit Card

HDFC Millenia Credit Card is a credit card issued by HDFC Bank. It has a rewards program and an introductory offer of 0% on balance transfers for 12 months. The features and benefits of this card are

  • Rewards program: You can earn points on every purchase you make with the card. The number of points earned depends on the type of transaction (e.g., cashback or air miles), but they don't expire as long as your account remains active and in good standing. Points can be redeemed for travel discounts, gift vouchers, merchandise or even cash back into your bank account via Paytm Wallet (1 point = Rs 1).
  • Introductory offer: There's no annual fee for the first year; after that it's Rs 2,500 per year unless you spend at least Rs 50K annually on this card during any given year (in which case there will be no charge). This means that if you're planning on getting this card primarily because it offers 0% interest rates during balance transfers, then remember that there may be other options available depending on how much money needs transferring over time - so make sure before committing!

2. Citi Cashback Credit Card

The Citi Cashback Credit Card is a good option if you want to earn rewards on your purchases. It offers 1% cashback on all your purchases, which can be redeemed as airtime or wallet balance. The card also comes with a complimentary insurance package that covers accidental death, disability and loss of job.

The eligibility criteria for this credit card are:

  • Minimum age 18 years
  • Maximum age 65 years (as on March 31, 2019)

3. Amex Membership Reward Credit Card

The Amex Membership Reward Credit Card is a rewards-based credit card that allows you to earn points for every purchase you make. You can redeem these points for travel, merchandise and gift cards.

The card offers several benefits such as:

  • No annual fee
  • 0% introductory interest rate on balance transfers for 6 months from the date of approval (after which the variable APR will be applicable)

4. Standard Chartered Digi Smart Credit Card

The Standard Chartered Digi Smart Credit Card is a rewards card that allows you to earn points on your purchases. You can redeem these points for discounts and cashback on your next purchase, or even airline tickets.

The card comes with a 0% introductory offer on balance transfers for 6 months, after which the interest rate will be applicable as per your credit limit. It has no annual fee or charges for late payment or cash withdrawals at ATMs outside India (except China), but there is a charge of 1% on all foreign currency transactions made in non-home country currencies (i.e., USD).

5. Amazon Pay ICICI Credit Card

Amazon Pay ICICI Credit Card is a rewards credit card that can be used at Amazon, as well as other online stores. The card offers cashback of up to 4% on all purchases made using the card. The features and benefits of this credit card are:

  • Cashback on every purchase made through Amazon Pay or any other digital wallet (3% for digital wallets).
  • No annual fee and no transaction fees for online transactions.
  • 2X reward points on all purchases made at restaurants, bars, cafes and food delivery services with a Zomato Gold membership (1X reward point otherwise).

With the rise of e-commerce, online shopping has become a part of our daily routine. As a result, credit cards that offer benefits for online shopping have become increasingly popular in India. Here are two of the best credit cards for online shopping in India:

6. SBI Simply Click Credit Card

The SBI Simply Click Credit Card is designed specifically for online shoppers. It offers a range of benefits, including:

  • 10X reward points on online spending with exclusive partners such as Amazon, BookMyShow, Cleartrip, Lenskart, and more.
  • 5X reward points on other online spends.
  • 1 reward point for every Rs. 100 spent.
  • No annual fee for the first year.
  • An annual fee of Rs. 499 from the second year onwards, which is waived if you spend more than Rs. 1 lakh in the previous year.

7. Amex SmartEarn Credit Card

The Amex SmartEarn Credit Card is another great option for online shoppers. It offers a range of benefits including:

  • 10X reward points on Flipkart and Uber spends.
  • 5X reward points on Amazon, Swiggy, BookMyShow, and more.
  • 1 reward point for every Rs. 50 spent.
  • No annual fee for the first year.
  • An annual fee of Rs. 495 from the second year onwards, which is waived if you spend more than Rs. 40,000 in the previous year.

Conclusion

Now that you have read through the guide, here are some key takeaways to help you get started:

  • Credit cards are an excellent way to build your credit history and improve your financial standing.
  • You should only apply for a credit card if you can afford the monthly payments and will use it responsibly.
  • The best way to decide which card is right for you is by comparing different offers and finding one that fits your needs and spending habits best.

When choosing a credit card, it's important to consider your individual needs and spending habits. Look for a card that offers rewards and benefits that align with your lifestyle and priorities. Additionally, make sure to read the terms and conditions carefully and understand any fees or charges associated with the card. By doing your research and choosing the right card, you can enjoy the benefits of online shopping while earning rewards and saving money.
